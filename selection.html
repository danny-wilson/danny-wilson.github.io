Research Interests: Detecting Selection

The aim of my research is to develop an evolutionary understanding of transmission dynamics and propensity for virulence in bacterial pathogens. The approach is an empirical one, through genomic analysis of natural populations. The central themes of my research programme are (i) transmission modelling, (ii) pathogen evolution and (iii) detecting selection. A strong theoretical basis and a focus on statistical methods development underpin my work.
